History of Samsung

Samsung was founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul as a trading company in Su-dong, Korea. Over the decades, it evolved into a global leader in electronics, semiconductors, and telecommunications. In the 1970s, Samsung ventured into the electronics industry and released its first black-and-white TV in 1970. By the 1990s, the company emerged as a pioneer in memory chips and mobile phones.

Samsung is known for its innovation and cutting-edge technology. From launching the first smartphone series to dominating the global market with advanced TVs, refrigerators, and wearables, Samsung continues to lead the electronics world. Today, it stands as one of the largest companies in the world, synonymous with quality and reliability.
